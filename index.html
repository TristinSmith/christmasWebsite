The History Of Christmas

Christmas is a Christian Holiday to celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ. December 25th was first identified as Jesus' birthday by Sextus Julius Africanus in 211 AD and became universally accepted afterward. The main figure of Christmas, Santa Claus, is based on the gift-giver from Church History, St. Nicholas, as well as influence from Father Christmas. Santa also has influence from the Dutch figure Sinterklass. Santa was first seen in a U.S. Newspaper in 1773 portrayed as a Jolly, Bearded, Plump Man in a Red Coat and Hat. He was also popularized by Coca-Cola commercials depicting Santa Claus. Santa has been a mainstay for Christmas ever since.
